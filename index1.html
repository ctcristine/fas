About Cesar Chavez

Cesar Chavez (1927-1993) was a civil rights activist and labor leader who co-founded the United Farm Workers (UFW) in 1962. He dedicated his life to fighting for the rights of farm workers who were often mistreated and underpaid. He organized protests, strikes, and boycotts to bring attention to the injustices faced by farm workers and to demand better working conditions and wages.

Accomplishments

Co-founded the United Farm Workers (UFW) in 1962

Organized the Delano grape strike, which lasted for five years and resulted in the first collective bargaining agreement for farm workers

Advocated for the rights of farm workers and helped improve their working conditions and wages

Used non-violent tactics such as fasting to protest against unjust treatment of farm workers

Quotes

"The fight is never about grapes or lettuce. It is always about people."

"Preservation of one's own culture does not require contempt or disrespect for other cultures."

"Once social change begins, it cannot be reversed. You cannot un-educate the person who has learned to read. You cannot humiliate the person who feels pride. You cannot oppress the people who are not afraid anymore."
